Technical aspect

The structure of my website is simple: a navigation bar and a body frame.
For the navigation bar, I created button to navigate to different webpages.
For the body frame, I first put an image and align it to the center of the box. After that, I created a table with one row and two columns, the first one is for the title (e.g. About) and the second for the description.
I made sure that I adjusted the paddings so that the text wouldn't stick to the edge of the box

I have nothing much to comment about how I choose the css selector for an element. Although most of my elements are unique, I included both attribute 'id' and 'class' in the assignment. Specifically, 'class' attribute is used for the wrapper, while the 'id' is used for the internal element of a wrapper such as headers.

Aesthetic quality

Axiom: Everything that is related to Violet Evergarden is beautiful regardless.

Font

My inspiration for this website is the anime Violet Evergarden, so the theme is a letter. I intended to choose an older, hand-writing font for the content in the webpages for the sake of theme illustration; however, it would be distracted if all the word are stylish, so I just used 'Luminari, fantasy' font for the headers and the buttons.

Color

Throughout the pages, I chose beige or light yellow as theme colors to match the background. The button in the navigation bar are painted with white and violet colors since they are *somehow* constrast and not contrast the theme color. Another reason is that they represent characteristic of the protagonist in the theme anime.

Structure

I rounded the corner the buttons and the body frame so that they won't look edgy. I also used the shadow effect to make they look more beautfiul

Accessibility

I made my website simple as much as possible with a navigator bar located to the top left of the page like many other websites.

My website is also friendly for blinded people as I didn't use much graphical objects, and all the images are noted with 'alt' attribute so that they can be read by voiceover. This feature may also support people with low-spec computers.
